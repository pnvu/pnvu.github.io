About Me

My name is Phan Nguyen Vu (Phan is my family name) from Vietnam. I graduated from Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology (HCMUT). As an undergraduate student, I joined BioMech Lab at HCMUT, and participated in various projects related to Brain-Computer Interface and Mechatronics System Design. After my graduation, I spent about 9 months pursuing research in Networking Lab, Sungkyunkwan University. My research there focused on Networking Systems, Wearable Devices, and Machine Learning.

I also had 8 months working as an embedded software engineer at Robert Bosch Engineering and Business Solutions Vietnam (RBVH), where I was a part of a team developing the interface between automotive systems and off-board testers in order to monitor and detect potential faults in the systems.

Selected Projects

Motor Imagery-based BCI

Implemented a non-invasive Brain-Computer Interface that translates motor imagery based EEG patterns into control commands used to activate movements (e.g., go forward and backward) of a three-wheeled mobile robot

Demo: click here

BCI-based IoT Application

Implemented an IoT application based on motor imagery based Brain-Computer Interface to help stroke patients inform their relatives by activating an alarm bell or sending SOS messages in case of emergency situations

For more details: click here

Tiva C Launchpad Kit

Collaborated with two other students on (1) developing the experiment kits and tutorials to help Mechatronics students to learn the basis of Tiva C Launchpad (produced by Texas Instruments), and (2) assisting my former advisor in writing the book "Application of microcontroller in mechatronics and robotics"

Agricutural IoT Application
