Brief Bio


He began his journey in 1994 in a little town located in the middle of Vietnam (there must be someone who chose it for him, he wonders about it sometimes but have yet found the answer). He spent his first 18 years to adapt the life and looked at the outside world through his old color television and books from his father's bookshelf. His opinion about this world changed time by time everytime he exposed to a new world from a book. He continued his life that way until one day in 2012, he decided to leave his hometown and went to Saigon (also known as Ho Chi Minh City nowadays) to experience the outside world. During six years living there, he had chances to learn many things and work in the field of Mechatronics Engineering under the advice of Dr. Quan Tuong Vo. After graduating, he decided to get himself out of his comfort zone. He chose Korea to continued discovering the world and writing his story. What will be next? Who knows?

Description

In this project, we developed experiment kits (containing basic modules like I/O with matrix led, LCD, interrupt, communication (UART and I2C), PWM, etc.) and tutorials to help students to be familiar with Tiva C Launchpad Kit (an ARM-based development kit). By learning the content of the tutorials and practicing with the experiment kits, the students will eventually grasp the fundamental of this type of microcontroller, easily learn other types and have the ability to apply knowledge as well as skills about MCU to their future projects related to mechatronics system or robotics.
All tutorials were synthesized to produce the book "Application of microcontroller in mechatronics and robotics", published under the name of our former advisor, Dr. Quan Tuong Vo.

Members

1. Thanh Thai Mai
2. Vu Nguyen Phan
3. Chau Thai Hua
4. Quan Tuong Vo

Content

Chapter 1: Introduction to Tiva C TM4C123GH6PM, Code Composer Studio (CSS) and the experiment kit.
Chapter 2: General-purpose input/output (GPIO).
Chapter 3: General-purpose input/output (GPIO) (continue).
Chapter 4: Interrupt.
Chapter 5: Timer and Systick.
Chapter 6: Analog-to-digital converter (ADC).
Chapter 7: UART communication.
Chapter 8: I2C communication.
Chapter 9: Pulse-width modulation (PWM).
Chapter 10: Controlling the DC motor position using PID controller.
Chapter 11: Controlling a stepper motor.
